FunNow
Instant Booking For Leisure Activity

Find target users’ need and improve the app and user satisfication

OVERVIEW
About the Project

FunNow is an App providing instant booking for entertainment and leisure activities, including travel, restaurants, massages and so on. In this project, we cooperated with Funnow and conducted UX research, such as interview, survey, comparative evaluation, heuristic evaluation and usability test. Through not only qualitative but also quantitative research to define the users’ need and optimize the app.

Timeline: 3.5 months

My Role: UX researcher, UX designer

THE CHALLENGE
Define Target Users/ Optimize App

Who are the target users

FunNow provides booking services for diverse activities. It has more than 40 million users from different backgrounds and with different needs. We should find out FunNow main users and realize their behavior, needs and the problems they face with.

How to optimize the interaction with FunNow

When we cooperate with FunNow, they are preparing to launch a new version app. Our challenge is to optimize the new version App based on users’ need and behavior.

PROCEDURE
Procedure
INTERVIEW
Interview

Although FunNow provides a variety of activities booking, we focused on two categories. One was “Restaurant/Bar service” that most people booking, and the other was ”massage service” which was unique than other similar booking apps. We issued questionnaires to the users of these two services through the app. This pilot survey helps us in the preliminary understanding of the users and being the base of our user research. We recruited the participants according to the results of the pilot survey. We interviewed users who fit the characteristics of FunNow’s target population. The interview results helped us to obtain qualitative data and decide what next steps to take in future phases of the project.

Participants

In total, twelve participants were interviewed. Among them, five for restaurants, five for bars and five for messages. There are three people aged 18-25 and 31-35 respectively, and nine people are aged 26-30. The participants included both students and professionals workers. All of them use FunNow at least three times per month.

Interciew scripts

1. Background: age, gender, job

2. Personal value:
⁃ Why you use FunNow?
⁃ what factors are you care about when booking?
⁃ what kinds of apps do you use?
⁃ what is your preference?

3. Behavior:
⁃ What did you booked last time you used FunNow?
⁃ How did you finish your booking?
⁃ How you felt in the process of booking?

INTERVIEW
Affinity Diagram

During the interviews, questions and follow-ups were tailored to the conversation at hand, either by adding additional questions and/or by the omitting ones from scripts. Following, we discussed the notes and used the affinity diagram to get an in-depth analysis of the qualitative research data. This session helped us to categorize the data of users’ motivation, behavior, issues, and problems they faced with.

INTERVIEW
Persona Spectrum

In order to sort the user type efficiently, we used persona spectrum to get a consensus of the persona characteristic. We defined some key characteristics of the difference between each users and draw it into spectrum line. We put users into a spectrum line and group the users who had similar characteristic and get the pattern of the same type of user. Finally, we got 5 pairs of user types and these user patterns would be used to construct our persona.

SURVEY
Survey - Quantitative Analysis

Before constructing personas, we conducted a quantitative survey to reduce sample error. We made the online questionaries to verify the reliability and accuracy of user patterns. The survey questions were specific to the insight we got from the interviews. The goal of the survey was to collect sample data representative of the population and to determine the characteristics, attitude and behavior of the target population.

Find key features that users really need

Through collect general data from population to find what users really care and scored the needs from both user and company aspect.

Relation between users’ needs and personas

We use Spearman correlation analysis to find the relation between the features and the value users care about. We also analyzed the users’ value and motivations.

Verify and revise the Personas

We made the objective statements of current users from quantitative data, and verified and revised our personas.

SURVEY
Key Findings

1. Frustration:
⁃ The bissgest problem for users when searching store is that there are less options for conditional filtering and the results are not as expected.
⁃ There are many problems with browsing the stores. Users can not quickly find and clearly understand the information.

2. User value:
⁃ Users are mainly care about the price, promotion and reviews.
⁃ When viewing the stores, users are mainly worry about the “quality and safety of the store” and “the consistency of pictures and physical object”.
⁃ Users want multiple choices of filters. The filtering factors that most users want are “reviews”, “average consumption,” “store ranking”, “attendance time” and “the location of the store”.
⁃ More than 60% users are willing to choose off-peak hours if there is promotion at that time.
⁃ More than 60% users would like to contact the store directly through the FunNow app.

COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS
Matrix Analysis

In this section, we aimed to shed light on areas in which FunNow excels relative to competition as well as areas in which FunNow falls short. In choosing competitive products, we sought apps that were similar to FunNow in at least one key area. We choose ten comparison apps and reduced all their features into seven categories. In each category, there have main features, sub-features, and extra functions. According to this feature categories, we made a comparison matrix to quickly review the pros and cons of all apps.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION
Heuristic Evaluation

As FunNow launched the new version app, we used the evaluation method outlined by Jakob Nielsen (Nielsen, 1994) to evaluate the new FunNow app. Heuristic evaluation is a design practice in which a group of usability experts evaluate software or websites in reference to standard set of usability principles or heuristics. The purpose of the exercise is to identify existing issues that violate these usability principles and come up with recommendations for how they can be fixed.

Heuristics Used

1. Visibility of system status

2. Match between system and the real world

3. User control and freedom

4. Consistency and standards

5. Error prevention

6. Recognition rather than recall

7. Flexibility and efficiency of use

8. Aesthetic and minimalist design

9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors

10. Help and documentation

Focus of Evaluation

Our team began by identifying the key tasks that users would need to accomplish in order for the website to be successful. The booking procedure has five major sections. Our primary focus was to evaluate key workflows for these sections and identify the heuristic breaches that are critical to FunNow usability. The key sections are as following:

1. Sign up and sign in

2. Browsing, includes direct browsing, filtering browsing, keyword search

3. View the store information and add interested store into “my collection”

4. Booking, includes first time booking, booking from collected stores and continuously booking twice in the same store

5. Manage the booking history

Individual Assessment

Each team member in our four-person team individually identified heuristic breaches in all five sections of the product. In the process we also assigned a severity rating based on a 1-4 numerical scale. After identifying each problem, the evaluator reviewed the nine other heuristics to see if multiple heuristics could support the finding, which simplified consolidation later in the process.

Combining Results and Assessing Severity

Once the individual assessments were done, we met as a team to discuss our individual findings and see if we could merge similarly identified problems. In the process, we also discussed and reached a consensus on severity ratings. Finally, we decided on the findings that we would focus on based on the importance of the use case and their severity. We deep-dived into our initial individual recommendations for these findings and researched and brainstormed ideas to address these issues. The sum of findings which was scored over 3 included in the recommendations below.

USABILITY TEST
Usability Test

The primary purposes of our usability testing as a means of providing a better assessment as to whether FunNow is meeting their goal of being an easy-to-use app for people of varying degrees, and to discover areas where the user experience can be improved. Methods such as heuristic evaluation and survey allowed to us to identify some major usability issues with FunNow. However, we wanted to confirm our findings and determine if additional usability problems are present. The exercise of usability testing provided qualitative data, which revealed new issues, as well as exemplified issues previously identified from heuristic evaluations and comparative analyses of FunNow.

Methodology

We started the process by designing various tasks that would help us to simulate actual app usage. We revisited our previous findings to formulate our tasks on actual app usage pattern. To collect demographic details of users, and report issues beyond specific taskcompletion, we created post-test questionnaires. We conducted a pilot test with two users and incorporated their feedback to improve our tasks and questionnaire. Based on our target population, we recruited our test participants through various methods. In preparation for the tests, we set up a suitable environment and to make sure our devices were working. After completing all tests, our group met to discuss and analyze the findings in detail. Each of these steps is explained in detail below.

Task Designing

We aimed at designing tasks that would allow users to utilize multiple functionalities of FunNow. Our previous research served to be an important anchor for task generation. By revisiting our findings from the above research, we identified certain usage patterns. Most of users have the need for temporary booking and the flagship concept of FunNow is to enjoy the services after 15 minutes; hence, we designed tasks that were to take approximately two to three minutes for completion. In total, we designed 6 task scenarios and deleted one after our pilot study. FunNow is mostly used to browse and to book services; therefore, our tasks involved searching stores and booking nd every task required users to achieve different goals. Our tasks were as follows:

1. Find the store you’re interested in and add to collection

2. Search for stores that meet your requirements, including the attendance of people, time and location.

3. Finish the booking and the payment, and then booking other services at the same store.

4. Write the review for the stores you have been to.

5. Find the store you have ever been to and book the services again.

Participants Selection

Our participants for the usability test were identified based on the target population of the app. Because the new version app launched recently, our participants included not only the potential user who never used FunNow before but also the users who have used the former version. A recruited questionnaire was created to help us find the participants. A total of six users responded to our usability testing requests and all of them were recruited for the usability tests.

The Setup

We aimed at selecting a physical setup that would make participants feel comfortable in performing the tasks and in giving us honest feedback. We decided to conduct tests in a closed room where participant will have no disturbance while performing the tasks. A digital video camera were used to capture the actions and responses of participants during each testing session. We provided mobile phones with android or ios systems according to the user's preference. Each of the three members of our group maintained a specific role throughout the usability testing: one moderated the testing and read the introduction and each of the tasks, one administered the questionnaires, and one took notes and time.

Analysis

After the successful completion of the tests, all of our group members met and analyzed the test scripts and screen recordings. We discussed in detail the difficulties faced by participants in completing the tasks and their confusion about particular UI element.Moreover, we examined the features that users liked and disliked about the website. We also referred back to the recommendations of our past heuristic analysis and survey report in examining similar feedbacks and confirmed findings. We also discussed the challenges that the moderator and the note taker faced while conducting the tests. This discussion helped us to revisit our test design and improve tests in the future. CONCLUSION
Short Term Recommendation

1. Registeration:
- Standardize the information format and indicate the users specification.

2. Browsing stores:
- Adjust the layout of the filter button to increase the efficiency of selecting filter.
- Display the review score so the users can view the key factors they care about without clicking into detail.

3. Filtering:
- Most of FunNow users have the need for temporary booking. Adding the time filter can avoid user changing back-and-forth.

4. Browsing detail information:
- The content layout should emphasize the factors that users care about, such as prices, promotions, and reviews.

5. Booking:
- Concentrate the booking process on one page and have user double check the booking.
- Take the info that needs to be filled in repeatedly as default when booking.
- Provide error detection and inform the correct format.

6. Writing reviews:
- Optimize the process of writing reviews, adding a button for the user to quickly reach to the destination.

CONCLUSION
Long Term Strategy

1. Make the keyword research more accurate and increase diversity tags.

2. Establish the regulation to evaluate the co-operated store to ensure the quality and strengthen the brand value.

3. Establish the online revising order system so that users could change their booking without calling customer service.

4. Promote FunNow to more cities and increase the partner stores.
