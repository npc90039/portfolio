Fridge to Go
Modular and Portable Fridge

Design a kitchen appliance for young tenants

OVERVIEW
About the Project

This project was based on a workshop held by the Academy of Arts & Design from Tsinghua University to design kitchen appliances or systems for young people who rent their on places. Four-person teams were created with assigned roles and responsibilities designing accessible kitchen appliances that meet users’ needs. We conducted user research to find design insight and prototyped our ideas. Finally, we propose a modular fridge with a connected app.

Timeline: 1 month advance preparation + 4 days workshop

My Role: As a designer, I analyzed the results from user research and defined our design features. I prototyped one unit of our modular fridge and created user flow, wileframes and high fidelity UI.

THE CHALLENGE

In densely populated cities, living spaces are limited. Identify the challenges encountered by house tenants and would like to cook by themselves and propose a solution that could make them have a better cooking experience.

USER RESEARCH
People Who Live in Limited Space

Whether renting a independent studio apartments, flats or shared houses, the kitchen equipments usually require a lot of space. However, only people with good economic conditions could afford to high quality kitchen environment. Thus, we decided to design a solution for those people who live in a limited individual space, such as studio apartments, domitories and shared-houses.

Participants

We interviewed 6 people, including students and working professionals and conducted a semi-structured interviewed which took about half an hour. Our interview script included asking their cooking procedure and habit, cooking appliances and the problems they faced with.

Environment Research

We surveyed the kitchen set in the market landscape, and the rental house environment from our interviewees. The cooking environment included the houses with a full kitchen set, the small houses with simple kitchen set, and the small suites with no kitchen equipment but simple appliances.

RESEARCH INSIGHT
Major Findings

Long lifespan of appliances

Most of the appliances are durable in good condition for more than ten years. However, users needs will be different as the role they play in life changes, and this kind of change is quickly.

Multiple application

Due to technical and hardware limitations, the function and appearance of home appliances are limited and resulted in energy consumption and space utilization inefficient. The appliances that meet different application scenarios are being popular.

The trend of smart home

The smart home product market has widely expanded. It allows you to have electronic things the way you like to have them. The customization makes the life easier and more convenient.

Rise of the sharing culture

The core concept of the sharing economy is the reuse of "idle resources." Thanks to the accessibility of the internet and mobile technology, managing share-based transactions has been easier.

Depict Our Target Users

Based on our interviews with 6 people, we basically divided them into two groups: people who live alone and people who live in shared-house, as these 2 types of people behave differently and also have different goals. Both of them will cook and want to make cooking more efficient.

THE SOLUTION
Modular and Portable Frige + Connected App

We design a modular fridge that includes a main body fridge with magnetic refrigeration technology and other expanded fridge units. Users can expand the storage space to meet their needs. Besides, the fridge is integrated with an App, the user could adjust the temperature, change the mode and manage food via App.

MODULAR FRIDGE
Concepts

1. Host fridge and individual units

2. Expanding storage space

3. Portable and customizable to meet different needs

4. Avaliable in different patterns to fit the house space

5. various modes to keep fridge in good condition
